§ 00 — Abstract

A new state of the art, by design.

Identifying promoters — the short stretches of DNA where transcription begins — is a foundational problem in prokaryotic genomics. DeepPro·v2 aggregates five DNA sequence foundation models into a single gated mixture-of-experts, fuses their representations with a learned anchor-prototype module, and attaches a differentiable motif grammar that scores the −35 and −10 promoter elements directly.

On held-out test data across all 23 species, DeepPro·v2 outperforms every published baseline. Beyond the leaderboard, its design is the point: a clean way to combine five complementary experts, and a grammar branch that recovers canonical transcription biology from binary labels alone.

§ 01 — Architecture

One sequence in. A gated bank of experts, a differentiable grammar, one decision out.

The input window is read in parallel by an expert bank of five pretrained genomic backbones, each with its own tokenisation. Learned gating coefficients weight the five expert views into a single fused representation — a gated mixture-of-experts optimised end-to-end — while a differentiable grammar branch reads the −35 and −10 promoter elements straight off the one-hot sequence and rejoins just before the classifier.

Adenine Cytosine Guanine Thymine Expert flow · gate · fusion Grammar bypass

Bar length beside each expert encodes its learned gating coefficient gk — a softmax over the expert bank that sums to one. These coefficients are optimised end-to-end with the rest of the network, blending the five complementary experts into a single fused representation rather than combining them by hand.

§ 02 — Method

Three additions, built on a stable core.

V2 extends a proven fusion core with three deliberate additions — a fifth expert, a differentiable grammar, and a learned gate.

  1. 01

    A fifth expert, matched to the domain

    ProkBERT-mini-c · +384-d view

    The four backbones are joined by a fifth, chosen to close a gap: a prokaryote-native, character-level language model pretrained directly on bacterial and archaeal genomes — the expert whose training domain sits closest to the task.

  2. 02

    A differentiable promoter grammar, on a bypass

    −35 · spacer · −10 → m·64

    A parallel branch reads the raw one-hot sequence through six differentiable experts, each a position-anchored grammar of −35 and −10 motifs. Its vector concatenates onto the fused representation — keeping the ensemble's story clean while adding an interpretable, biology-shaped signal.

  3. 03

    Fusion as a gated mixture-of-experts

    Σ gₖ·vₖ · learned gating

    Fusion is cast as a gated mixture-of-experts: learned gating coefficients weight the five expert views, mean-initialised and regularised for stability, so the blend is optimised end-to-end rather than fixed by hand.

Evaluation protocol

Five-fold cross-validation on each genome's training pool, plus a separate held-out test set per genome. The final prediction is an ensemble vote across the five folds, and every genome trains its own model.

Stage-0 pre-training · TAPT

Backbone pre-training runs on a separate merged 23-genome corpus — 227,089 train / 25,512 val / 64,122 test windows (cleaned) — kept apart from the per-genome benchmark scored above.

Window counts are per-genome totals (train + test) and sum exactly to 321,858. The Stage-0 corpus is a separate cleaned split, not part of the per-genome evaluation.
